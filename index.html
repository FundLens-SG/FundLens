Quick Start Guide

1
Add Holdings — Tap + to add a holding. Choose the type (Unit Trust, Stock, ETF, REIT, Bond, or ILP), then search by name. Fields auto-fill from our database. Or tap 📷 to scan a document — AI reads your policy statements, fund reports, or portfolio screenshots and fills in the fields automatically.
2
Enter Values — Input current value and starting value (cost basis) for each holding. Tick USD for US-dollar holdings — it auto-converts to SGD.
3
Review Analysis — The Overview tab shows portfolio composition, risk score, and P/L. Returns shows projected growth. Health flags concentration risks.
4
For ILP Policies — Select "ILP" type, add sub-funds with allocations. Enter premium details for yield projection.
5
Save & Share — Data auto-saves to your browser. Tap 💾 to download a portable HTML file you can email to clients or keep as a backup. Use 📄 to generate a client report.
6
Load Saved Files — Tap 📂 to reload a previously saved FundLens® file. This imports the client’s holdings back into the live tool with the latest fund database, live stock feeds, and updated analysis engine — something the offline saved file alone cannot do.

Data Sources & Compilation
All fund, ETF, and stock information in FundLens® has been compiled from publicly available sources using Claude AI (Anthropic). Fund and ETF data, including risk ratings, are sourced from the iFAST repository and official insurer websites (AIA, Great Eastern, Prudential, Manulife, NTUC Income). Data may change over time and may not reflect the most current information.

Risk Analysis Methodology
Risk analysis of individual stocks is performed using a quantitative formula based on CFA-standard metrics (Beta, Volatility, Debt-to-Equity ratio, and sector-adjusted qualitative factors). This methodology is one of many used by professional analysts and may not represent the most current or comprehensive way to assess risk. Portfolio-level risk uses a Markowitz mean-variance framework with concentration adjustments.
